Issue to Action @ Generation Citizen Civic Tech Challenge

In November, I attended Generation Citizen’s Civic Tech Challenge. Generation Citizen is a nationwide non-profit group that empowers teenagers to become engaged in politics and civic activity. They organize outreach events and also coordinate volunteer-led “Democracy Classes” in schools. They held a hackathon -- the “Civic Tech Challenge” -- in Cambridge where participants were tasked with building technological tools to enhance and support civic problem-solving. In particular, my group’s task was to build a tool to help would-be young activists decide how and where to allocate their problem-solving energy. This tool was to be used in classrooms to help Generation Citizen students decide which issues to engage with.

My teammates and I felt that it was very important for young activists to know when there is a critical mass of people who care about similar issues. When that activist discovers a critical mass of similarly minded people, they need to educate themselves about the issue, and then take action. To facilitate this process, our team prototyped a web application that would connect concerned individuals with similarly minded activists, educational materials, and actionable activities. Screenshots of the basic flow can be seen below.

In the first view, users are prompted to enter a statement about their concern or issue. We choose the prompts carefully, hoping to preclude flippant entries and encourage people to think about the root of their cause. In the second view, users will see all other statements that are related to their issue. We extract key words from each users statement and use these to tag and categorize the statements. Users can filter results on different key words or find other users within a certain radius. On the right-hand side of the screen, users can discover relevant action items (which are set dynamically based on the search results and filters) or educational materials about the issue they’ve entered.

After the daylong hackathon, our project won the usability award for ease of use, intuitiveness, and appeal to a young generation of users. Though we just completed a prototype at the hackathon, we are planning to continue work on this project at Code for Boston hack nights. The full prototype can be experienced at www.cajaffe.com/issue-to-action.
